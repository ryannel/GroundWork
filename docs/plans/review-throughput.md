## 0. Read this first — the mental model

The bottleneck is human fatigue during review, and the inventory shows the fatigue is not the *amount* of information — it is four structural misallocations:

1. **Uniform density at the sign-offs.** The engine already knows which decisions genuinely needed the owner (it asked them), which findings the reviewer forced, and which trace checks a gate verified mechanically — then the sign-off walk flattens all of it back into an everything-at-the-same-depth re-walk. Slice proofs get milestone-grade scrutiny even though the process itself declares slice steering free and unrecorded. Attention is spent where it steers *least*.
2. **A dead zone in the acceptance chain.** The owner approves plain-language Proof-of-work prose at decomposition (the right artifact, at the right time — that is the design-up-front leverage this method is built on), then touches acceptance again only at the milestone-close drive. In between, the prose→red-stub materialization is a single-agent judgment nobody re-presents; the milestone stub template ships its user-observable proof *commented out*; the readiness gate checks only that a test file is *named*; and the machine evidence that the tests actually bite (`mutate`, `honesty`, `wiring`, `tokens`) dies in the agent's terminal. A green board can silently prove less than what was signed.
3. **The durable channel is dead and the ephemeral channel is overloaded.** The docsite — the designated free-exploration and Protocol 12 render surface — must be manually booted, goes stale at boot time (`sync-live-bets` runs once at `predev`), has no program dashboard and no "waiting on you" page, and erases the bet's trail at archive. Meanwhile chat carries everything: the milestone boundary stacks snapshot + walkthrough + postmortem + ratification + dispositions + the manual drive into one scrolling mega-moment, full snapshots re-serve unchanged tables, and re-presentations never lead with what changed.
4. **The host's own review machinery is half-wired.** Protocol 12 (inline-comment review at a canonical path) covers five artifacts but not change proposals, amendments, mid-delivery slice authoring, or revise-cap rulings; no rule anywhere mandates a delta on re-presentation; structured elicitation (Claude Code's question tool; the shipped `./dev` prompt toolkit) is never used, so batch-shaped rulings (ratify/veto queues, ledger fills) run as serial chat.

The plan's economics, in the owner's own words: **pay attention up front where it steers (design, the milestone ladder, the agreed front-door cases), make the in-between evidence glanceable rather than mandatory, and let review throughput come from choreography — right surface, right order, right density — not from weakened gates.** Every gate, lens, verdict grammar, and wire format in Protocols 8/9 stays byte-identical; the sealed-prose regime for acceptance tests (the Regime-3 trade that deliberately killed verbatim assertion review) stands.

The attention ledger is honest about its own additions. Exactly three slices add *required* attention, each bought with a larger removal: the rendered token sheet (A6) replaces approving visual design blind and correcting it at milestone close; the new-rung slice presentation (D2) replaces a today-silent approval the owner would have wanted (F12) and folds into an existing pause; the validation drive checklist (B5) replaces the never-ticked checkboxes and the archaeology of reconstructing the agreed cases. Every other new surface — the proof board, the in-flight dashboard, "waiting on you", the delivery record, the bet panel, the walkthrough appendix, the ladder page — is explicitly glance-optional: pointed at once, never required reading, always a replacement for something costlier (narration, scrollback, commit spelunking).

One inherited debt is discharged first: user-legibility still owes its live proof (a delivery sim judged with the legibility rubric; a pitch→inline-comment→revision→approval round trip). Wave 0 runs it — baseline before build.

## 2. Workstream A — the attention contract (design-up-front, two densities)

The design phase is where owner attention buys the most; this workstream concentrates it on contested decisions and renders the rest as compressed, expandable evidence.

**A1 — Two-density sign-off walks.**
Rewrite the Transition sections of `02-design.md` (:177–184) and `03-decomposition.md` (:246–255). During drafting, the agent keeps the decision trail in engine state — but **shaped so downstream pending counts stay truthful**: a decision the user already answered live is recorded as `decisions add` *immediately followed by* `decisions ratify --id --response "<their verbatim answer>"` (never left pending); an applied reviewer 🔴 is recorded via `findings add` + disposition `fixed` (it is a finding, not an open ruling); only genuinely open deferrals stay `pending`. Nothing recorded for display may sit in the pending queue past the sign-off. The sign-off then opens with the ledger at **two densities of its own**: items the user personally ruled on render as one-line recaps (question + their answer — re-walking them at full depth re-spends attention already paid); full density is reserved for what they have *not* seen — persona deferrals, reviewer-🔴 applications, and a **caller-side always-full class independent of the agent's self-report**: contract deltas (endpoint shapes and error cases in `03-api-design.md`), one-way-door/irreversible choices, and anything the pitch's Stakes names as blast radius — these are walked with alternatives-considered, not just what-was-verified, because a contested choice the agent made confidently and the reviewer PRESENTed is exactly the one nothing else would surface. Everything the PRESENT verdict covered mechanically compresses to one line per section naming what was verified and by which check, plus the canonical docsite pointer (phrased per the C1 liveness rule), expandable on request. In the decomposition walk, milestone Proofs of work keep assertion-grade pacing (they are what the `bet/<slug>/approved` tag signs) — **superseded in Wave 2 by B1's board-row walk, one traversal, never both**; the first milestone's slice files present as one scan table (id, capability gist, proof gist, owner service) with an invitation to open any on the docsite — per the existing rule that slice steering is free.
*Files:* `src/hidden-skills/groundwork-bet/workflows/02-design.md`, `03-decomposition.md`.
*Accept:* grep — assertion-grade scrutiny applies to milestones only and the Transition instructs the slice scan table; a decomposition-sign-off sim shows ledger-first (recaps one-line, unseen items full), slices-as-table; the sim includes a contested-but-unasked API decision and asserts it renders at full density; after sign-off, `decisions pending` lists only genuine deferrals.

**A2 — Advisories lead, in context; withheld advisories persist.**

Design-phase 🟡s surface at the *design* sign-off (today they are held until decomposition — `02-design.md:113`). Withheld-for-length advisory topics are recorded via `findings add` so they land in committed engine state instead of vanishing. `groundwork-review`'s output contract is byte-untouched.
*Files:* `02-design.md`, `03-decomposition.md`.
*Accept:* a sim with a withheld-advisories review return produces matching `findings.json` entries and section-anchored advisories; grep confirms design 🟡s are no longer deferred to decomposition.

**A3 — The milestone ladder gets its diagram.**

**A5 — Product-choice findings are never self-resolved invisibly.**

## 3. Workstream B — the acceptance-test throughline (the proof board)

The agreed front-door cases become one generated page the owner tracks from sign-off to the drive — derived from committed truth like `status --write`, never a signature, never a manifest, every in-between touchpoint a glance. **The board is never hand-edited and carries no approval or confirmation state — ever.** Owner confirmations live in engine state (B5); the board only renders derived truth.

**B1 — `groundwork proofs` — the proofs-at-a-glance board.**
New engine module `lib/bet-proofs/` reusing `lib/bet-status/derive.js`'s parsers (extended to capture the Consumer line, acceptance-criteria bullets, the Proves line, and the Test-file path; both `- [ ]` and plain bullets). One row per agreed case: milestone / consumer / the case verbatim / proof path. New `proofs` verb in `bin/groundwork.js` (`--bet`, `--json`, `--write`); `--write` renders `docs/bets/<slug>/proofs.md` whole with the `status.md` GENERATED-header discipline — under `docs/bets/<slug>/`, so the docsite renders it with zero pipeline change and `sync-live-bets` transports it for worktree/branch bets untouched. Wiring: `03-decomposition.md` Transition runs `proofs --write` after PRESENT; **the board-row walk supersedes A1's prose proof-walk wording — one traversal: the rows carry the assertion-grade pacing, the tree is drill-down and the inline-comment surface only** (the Protocol 12 host pass still runs on the tree). Parser degrades per-row (a malformed milestone renders "(no criteria parsed — see the milestone page)"), never fails the board.
*Files:* `lib/bet-proofs/index.js` (NEW), `lib/bet-status/derive.js`, `bin/groundwork.js`, `03-decomposition.md`, `tests/cli/test_bet_proofs.py` (NEW).
*Accept:* fixture bet with both bullet forms → one row per criterion; `--write` is byte-idempotent; page passes the engine-vocabulary denylist; the Transition names exactly one primary walk artifact (grep); existing cli suite green.

**B2 — The front-door stub ships active, and a commented-out proof becomes an honesty lead.**

## 4. Workstream C — the docsite becomes the standing review hub

One idempotent verb away, self-refreshing while it runs, honest about mirror freshness, and carrying the two pages worth a glance: the in-flight dashboard and "waiting on you".

## 8. Sequencing and gates

Waves are independent PR sets; each is bet-sized and lands whole. Skill prose is Tier-1 clean-replace **except** the engineer skills, which propagate via the update lane's `engineer-skills` reconcile family (E7); engine slices ship in the npm package; generator/dev-cli slices carry their migration or `[no-migration]` annotation per the shipping rules, and every `cli-src/` change rebuilds the bundle (`npm run build:dev-cli`).

**Wave 0 — discharge the inherited proof debt.** Run the owed user-legibility live sims (delivery sim judged with the legibility rubric; a pitch→inline-comment→revision→approval round trip) via the sim harness. Their findings calibrate Wave 1's prose — build on measured reality, not assumption. *Gate:* both sims run and graded; findings filed.

**Wave 1a — protocols: the host-native review layer.** D1–D7 (Protocol 13, delta-on-re-present, P12 widening, P9-preserving batch rule, persona wiring, the medium map). *Gate:* `./dev lint skills` + `./dev check sync-anchors` clean; byte-diffs of Protocol 8/9 grammar and the Protocol 9 two-path procedure; a sim exercising one comment-resolution → delta-led re-present cycle, one batched ratification, and the taught-then-compressed medium map.

**Wave 1b — choreography: the attention contract and the pauses.** A1–A6, E1, E4, B6 (depends on 1a: E1's ratification stage cites Protocol 13; A1's pointers cite the liveness phrasing which lands fully in 3a — until then the fallback "path + boot hint" wording applies). *Gate:* lint + sync-anchors clean; a delivery sim shows two-density sign-offs (with the contested-unasked-decision check), the staged six-part postmortem inside its caps, and truthful pending counts after sign-off; sim-judge rubric gains the delta-re-present, trailer-presence, and pause-agenda dimensions; read-back — no sandbox-product specifics in any edit.

**Wave 2 — engine: the proof board and the instant, actionable status.** B1, B2 (lib/bet-honesty half), B3, C4, C5 (lib half — derive.js cache write + shared `--with-proofs` derivation), E2, E3, E6. *Gate:* new cli suites (`test_bet_proofs`, `test_status_delta`, `test_state_since`, `test_status_queue_footer`) + full `tests/cli` green; the one-suite-per-slice-close tripwire; `./dev ci` green; changelog lines annotated.

**Wave 3a — the docsite hub.** C1, C2, C3 (+`gw-live-first-bets-meta`), C7. *Gate:* generation + contracts + migration fixtures green; sandbox acceptance — boot via `./dev docs`, live worktree-edit pickup without restart, three-source dashboard rows (worktree / branch-only / checkout-resident quick bet), mirror banner, `next build` with `_live` absent; grep — no dangling `pnpm dev` boot lines.

**Wave 3b — board wiring, dev-CLI, templates.** B2 (template half), B4, B5, C5 (dev-cli half), C6, E5, E7. *Gate:* generation tests + dev-cli unit tests green; a delivery sim shows the board at Step 0.5 / slice close / postmortem (in its E1 slots), the drive-before-archive validation with engine-state confirmations, and the verification roll-up; migration-coverage gate clean.

**Done means:** the owner's attention lands where it steers — a design sign-off that opens with the decisions that needed them and walks the one-way doors with alternatives in hand; a decomposition approved from a diagrammed ladder and a proof board they can reread on the docsite at any moment; the board tracking those same agreed cases red→green with honest evidence that they bite; every pause opening with what changed and what needs a ruling — batched, structured, truthfully counted, and durable on a page that is one verb away and actually current; and the bet's trail, including the owner's own rulings, surviving its own archive. The sim judge's legibility dimensions plus the new acceptance checks make the next regression a red gate, not a tired user.
