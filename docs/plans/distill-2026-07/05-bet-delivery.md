## 1. Intention

The section exists to make an agent deliver product changes **honestly at calibrated depth**: a user-approved prose contract (what will be proven, at whose front door) is authored before code; delivery is an orchestration in which a thin driver dispatches disposable workers and strong independent review gates every diff; and nothing quietly weakens the contract — every change to *what is proven* leaves a recorded trail. Three lanes (patch · quick bet · bet) are calibrations of one spine (Frame→Prove→Build→Check→Record), sized at the orchestrator's Work Intake.

Per-file intention map:

- **`instructions.md`** — route a session to the right phase file from pitch `status`/`track` frontmatter, and give the agent just enough shape of the whole lifecycle to make routing and resume decisions. It is a router, not a doctrine store.
- **`workflows/00-quick.md`** — deliver one small capability in one sitting by compressing discovery/design/decomposition into a single AI-authored, user-approved plan that the *unchanged* delivery machinery then executes. "Compress the prose, never the gate."
- **`workflows/01-discovery.md`** — produce a pitch that bounds the bet: real problem, falsifiable signal, opportunity-cost appetite, stakes, explicit no-gos; seeded from prior signals (discovery notes, patch clusters, maturity roadmap, capability ledger).
- **`workflows/02-design.md`** — lock the design contract (UI first, then headless core at field-level fidelity) so decomposition and delivery never invent shapes; retire real unknowns with throwaway POCs (Step 1.92) scoped against prior proof.
- **`workflows/03-decomposition.md`** — turn the design into the full milestone ladder of front-door headline proofs (only the first rung sliced), get the user's assertion-grade approval, and record that baseline so delivery can be held to it.
- **`workflows/04-delivery.md`** — the driver's spine: materialize the red board, dispatch a fresh worker per slice, review each diff through independent lenses, commit each slice as its record, close milestones at the front door with visual/experience gates, run postmortems that course-correct and author the next rung — routing every contract change through Amendment/Change Navigation, never a silent edit.
- **`workflows/05-validation.md`** — close the loop: whole-bet reconciliation, capture the served contract as canon, ledger rows, archive, owner drives the real product, back-feed upstream docs, retrospective, one user-gated trunk merge.
- **`briefs/slice-worker.md`** — a cold-loaded worker implements one slice to green *honestly* inside the locked design, rolls out permanent tests, self-reconciles, reports, never commits, escalates instead of forcing a green.
- **`briefs/blind-reviewer.md` / `edge-case-tracer.md` / `acceptance-auditor.md` / `coverage-auditor.md`** — four per-slice lenses that each catch one failure class (context-free correctness; unhandled paths; design conformance + honesty; test comprehensiveness + assertion bite), reporting findings for the driver to triage.
- **`briefs/experience-auditor.md`** — a milestone/bet-scope designer's judgment over the assembled running product: patterns complete, no dead ends, states present, joy bar.
- **`templates/bet-progress-test.md`** — calibrate the driver materializing the red board: target-state, system-level, lean headline suite, explicit-red stubs, naming, fixtures, screenshot capture.
- **`templates/pitch.md`, `decomposition/*`, `technical-design/*`, `change-proposal.md`** — output skeletons that carry the shared identifiers (frontmatter keys, `Test file:` paths, Proof-of-work slots) downstream readers depend on.
- **`groundwork-patch/instructions.md`** — the pressure valve: one bounded change at small-work speed, still tested and honest, landed as one stamped commit (`Lane: patch` / `Area:`) so clusters surface as bet signal.

This is the single highest-value fix: without it the designed flow dead-ends at Step 0. *Improves delivery:* the ratchet the whole integrity story hangs on actually exists for full bets.

**M2 — Give slices their `Surface` field (BET-2).** Add `**Surface:** core \| <registry-slug>` to `templates/decomposition/slice.md` and to 03 Step 4's slice-spec list (one line, "registry projects only; omit when no `docs/surfaces.md`"), matching 00-quick and the readiness checklist strings exactly. *Improves delivery:* core-before-surface sequencing and the ledger landing column become derivable; the readiness gate stops being unsatisfiable.

Then: 03 Step 3 keeps only the authoring-time application (how to *write* a proof that satisfies invariant 1, with the enumerated tells appearing once, here — this is where authors need calibration); 04 §2 keeps a two-line check pointing at the tells in 03; the acceptance-auditor keeps its operational honesty bullets (a cold-loaded brief must self-carry them — that is the one legitimate duplicate); delete the restatements from `instructions.md`'s current run-ons, `milestone-index.md`, `bet-progress-test.md` (pointer to 03), and the 04 postmortem Q1 (keep the *milestone-level erosion* insight, drop the re-enumeration). *Improves delivery:* one canonical phrasing to update; agents stop reading the same paragraph six times and the phase-specific applications become visible instead of drowned.
